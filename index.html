Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about scooter hire in Airlie Beach

Do I need a motorcycle licence to ride your mopeds?

No! All our mopeds are 50cc, which means you can ride them on a regular Australian car licence (full or provisional). International licence holders can also ride — just bring your licence with you. This makes our Airlie Beach moped hire the easiest way to get around the Whitsundays.

How much does it cost to hire a scooter in Airlie Beach?

We offer the best prices for scooter hire in Airlie Beach. Electric scooters start from just $30 per day ($120/week) and mopeds from $45 per day ($250/week). All prices include helmets. We also offer flexible weekly instalment payments for longer rentals.

Do you deliver the scooter to me?

Yes! We offer free pickup and drop-off around Airlie Beach. Whether you're staying at a hotel, hostel, or Airbnb, we'll bring the moped or scooter right to your door. Just let us know your accommodation when you book.

What's included with the rental?

Every rental includes helmets, a full tank of fuel (mopeds cost about $5 to refuel), and a walkthrough on how to ride. Our mopeds are automatic so they're super easy — if you can ride a bicycle, you can ride one of our scooters around Airlie Beach and the Whitsundays.

Can I ride the moped with a passenger?

Our mopeds can carry two people, but keep in mind they're 50cc so they're not great on steep hills with two riders. For flat roads and around Airlie Beach they're perfectly fine for two. The electric kick scooter is single-rider only.

How do I book?

Simply choose a scooter or moped above, click "Request Booking", select your dates, and fill out the rental agreement — all online. We'll confirm your booking and arrange pickup. It's the fastest way to book scooter hire in Airlie Beach.

Where can I ride?

Anywhere on public roads! Our mopeds are fully registered for Queensland roads. Popular rides from Airlie Beach include the scenic route to Shute Harbour, cruising along the Whitsunday coast, and exploring the backstreets of town. They're the best way to see the Whitsundays at your own pace.
